Is the numerator a subset of the denominator?



If this indicator is a proportion, the numerator will generally be a sub-set of the denominator.

If the indicator is an incidence in a standard population, or ratio, the numerator and denominator will generally be calculated separately.

Example where an numerator is a sub-set of a denominator:
”This indicator calculates the percentage of women who were smokers at the time of delivery, from the number of maternities.”

Example where an indicator is not a sub-set of a denominator:
”This indicator measure the potential years of life lost per 100,000 registered patients, from conditions which are usually treatable.
